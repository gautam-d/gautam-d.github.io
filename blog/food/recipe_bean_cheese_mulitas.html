~ Recipe ~ Bean & Cheese Mulitas ~


Mulitas are basically mini-quesidillas made with small-sized tortillas. I usually make 2 variations of bean & cheese mulitas - one with white cannelini beans, the other with black beans. The method of preparing them is the same, the ingredients are just slightly different.


You can also find my recipe for egg & cheese mulitas here >>.

Servings:

 2  (2 mulitas per person, 4 total)

~~~~~~~~~~

Step 1:


In a saucepan, add cooking oil and heat on high. Add onions and sautee until golden brown.


Step 2:


Next, add red bell peppers (and corn, if used) and sautee until slightly charred.


Step 3:


Reduce heat to medium. Add white canneleni beans (or black beans), salt, black pepper, peri-peri spice blend (or cayenne pepper) and lemon juice. Stir and cook for 3-5 minutes.


Step 4:


Switch off heat. Add spinach leaves (or italian parsley / cilantro) and stir once more. Cover saucepan to keep the bean and vegetable filling warm.


Step 5:


On a large frying pan, place 2 corn tortillas and heat on medium-high. Once tortillas have browned on one side, flip over and brown the other side.


Step 6:


Bring heat to low. On one tortilla, add 1/8 cup of shredded cheese. Then, add quarter of the prepared bean and vegetable filling on top of the cheese. Next, add quarter amount of the avaocado pieces. Finally, place the other tortilla on top.


Step 7:


Continue to heat mulitas until the cheese melts, but make sure not to burn the tortilla. Then, flip the mulitas once to heat other side.


Step 8:


Remove from frying pan and cut mulitas in half.


Step 9:


Repeat steps 5 - 8 with the remaining ingredients to make 4 mulitas in total. Serve and enjoy!
